Perpetually Against Humanity is an IRC game based upon the card game Cards Against Humanity™, but designed to be played over a long period of time (forever) with a changeable group of people.

Cards Against Humanity

Cards Against Humanity is:

  • A trademark of Cards Against Humanity LLC; their content is licensed under a Creative Commons license which is what allows this IRC game to exist. This project is otherwise completely unrelated to Cards Against Humanity and is not endorsed by Cards Against Humanity LLC.
  • A party game for horrendous people. In order to win you pretty much have to construct outrageous politically incorrect plays. If that is likely to upset you then you won't like the IRC version either.
  • A great game which is much more fun in person than any IRC version can ever be, so if you like this then you should definitely buy it.

Player Documentation

This page is aimed at people who are wanting to play Perpetually Against Humanity (PAH).

You can play right now by joining us in ##pah on Freenode.

If you're wanting to install it in order to run your own then please refer to the GitHub repository for downloads and full instructions.

Throughout this documentation we will assume that:

  • The bot's IRC nickname is P_A_H (which is what it is on Freenode). If you're playing in someone else's game then it will be a different nickname.
  • The channel we're playing in is ##pah (which is one of the channels on Freenode). If you see the bot in a different channel then just use that one.

This section covers all the commands you will need to use to play the game. See the Complete List of Commands for a few more commands that may be useful.

Basic rules

It's very much like Cards Against Humanity. If you aren't familiar with Cards Against Humanity, however, this is how it goes:

  • There's a Black Card with some text on it.
  • Each player has ten White Cards, each of which have an answer on them.
  • Players choose which White Card(s) they think will go with the Black card to make the funniest play.
  • One player is called the Card Tsar; they do not get to play a White Card. Instead they get to pick the funniest play once everyone else has made their play.
  • Whoever was responsible for the funniest play wins one Awesome Point.
  • Someone else becomes Card Tsar, everyone's hands are topped back up to ten White Cards, and a new Black Card is drawn.

There's some logs of a real game to give you more of an idea.

This game is meant to be played slowly, while you are on IRC anyway. If you want a real-time IRC game of Cards Against Humnaity then you should run one of the many bots that already exist for this, or download your own copy of PAH and configure it for quick games.

Joining an existing game

Use the deal me in command in the channel:

16:28:17 < Diglett> P_A_H: deal me in
16:28:18 <+P_A_H> Diglett: Nice! You're in!

You can be in multiple games at once.

Seeing your hand

There should always be 10 White Cards in your hand. To get a list of them at any time use the hand command in private message with P_A_H:

Making your play

Use the play command in private message with P_A_H:

11:31:44 <Diglett> play 10
11:31:58 <P_A_H> Thanks. So this is your play for ##pah:
11:31:59 <P_A_H> → Why am I sticky? Coat hanger abortions.

You can miss off the "play " part and just give a number between 1 and 10.

If The Black Card has two blanks then you'll need to specify two White Cards in the order you want them to appear:

11:31:44 <Diglett> 7 3
11:31:58 <P_A_H> Thanks. So this is your play for ##pah:
11:31:59 <P_A_H> → If God didn't want us to enjoy being rich, he wouldn't have given us children on leashes.

You can keep changing your play until the last person has made their play.

Leaving the game

As long as you can do your duty (make a play or pick the winner) within the game's turnclock then there is no need to resign. The turnclock in ##pah is 3 days.

If you know you'll be gone from the game for longer than this then it is polite to resign, so as not to hold the game up for everyone else.

To resign, use the resign command in the channel:

17:31:49 < Diglett> P_A_H, resign
17:31:50 <+P_A_H> Diglett: Okay, you've been dealt out of the game.
17:31:50 <+P_A_H> Diglett: If you want to join in again later then type "P_A_H: deal me in"

If you don't do your duty before the turnclock expires then P_A_H will forcibly resign you from the game.

Getting a reminder of the Black Card

P_A_H tells you the Black Card every time it changes but if you've forgotten then you can get a reminder at any time by using the black command in private message with P_A_H:

16:37:15 <graveler> black
16:37:15 <P_A_H> Current Black Card for game in ##pah:
16:37:15 <P_A_H> → In his newest and most difficult stunt, David Blaine must escape from _____.
16:37:15 <P_A_H> The current Card Tsar is Diglett.
16:37:15 <P_A_H> Use the "Play" command to make your play!

Complete List of Commands

Public channel commands

  • status

    Report the status of the game currently playing in the channel, if any.

    If a game is currently playing then this will say:

    • Who we're waiting on (either to play their turn, or to pick the winner if they're the Card Tsar);
    • Who the current Card Tsar is;
    • The text of the current Black Card.

  • scores

    stats

    Displays:

    • Who's active in the game and what their scores are;
    • The top three all-time points-scorers for this game, including ties.

  • dealmein

    deal me in

    Join in to the currently-active game. You'll immediately receive a hand and a reminder of the current Black Card by private message. You can play your hand immediately.

    If the game already has 20 players then you will need to wait until one of them resigns.

    If the game now has enough players to start then it will start.

  • resign

    dealmeout

    deal me out

    retire

    quit

    Resign from the currently-active game. Your scores will still be kept and you can join in to the game again later on. If this takes the number of active players below 4 then the game will be paused until someone joins again.

    Your hand of White Cards including any White Cards you already played are discarded.

    If you are the current round's Card Tsar then play will move on without any Awesome Points being awarded. If the hand was complete (all players made their play and plays were announced in the channel) then these White Cards will be discarded and new White Cards drawn. Otherwise played White Cards will be returned to players' hands.

  • winner

    Used by the Card Tsar to indicate that the play numbered is the one they think is best.

    This command can be simply abbreviated as a single digit, as it will be obvious from the context what is meant.

  • plays

    Repeat the list of plays for the completed hand, if for some reason you missed it.

Private commands

  • [#channel] status

    Identical to the public status command, but in private in case you don't want to bother the channel with the output.

  • [#channel] scores

    [#channel] stats

    Identical to the public scores command, but in private in case you don't want to bother the channel with the output.

  • [#channel] black

    Repeat the text of the current Black Card.

  • [#channel] hand

    [#channel] list

    Provides a numbered list of the White Cards in your hand.

  • [#channel] play <number>

    [#channel] play <number> <number>

    [#channel] play <number>,<number>

    [#channel] play <number> and <number>

    [#channel] play <number> & <number>

    <number>

    <number> & <number>

    Play the numbered White Card for this round. Some rounds require two White Cards, so they will be played in the order specified.

    The bot will repeat back to you the current Black Card and your proposed answer.

    You may alter your play as many times as you like until every active player has made their play, at which point the plays will be revealed to the channel and may not be changed.

    Where a channel will not be required, just saying one or two numbers (without the "play" prefix) is an acceptable shortcut.

  • [#channel] plays

    Repeat the list of plays, if for some reason you missed it.

  • pronoun <pronounm>

    By default the bot uses the "their" pronoun:

    16:26:37 <+P_A_H> dutchie: We're just waiting on dave2 to make their play.
    17:16:47 <+P_A_H> 3 people just played! We're just waiting on dg to make their play.

    If you would prefer for the bot to use a different pronoun in relation to you, use this command to set it. This will apply in all games that you are in.

Glossary

Black Card

There's one Black Card per round, which everone sees. It will have zero, one or two blank spaces on it which players fill by choosing from their White Cards. If the Black Card has zero blanks then there's an implicit blank right at the end.

White Card

Each player has ten White Cards at all times. Also called answer cards. White Cards are chosen to fill in the blanks on the Black Cards.

Round

A round starts when a new Black Card is drawn, proceeds while all players make their play, and ends when the Card Tsar picks the funniest play. The winner then gets one Awesome Point.

Card Tsar

One player is the Card Tsar. The Card Tsar does not play White Cards. Instead, the Card Tsar chooses which play is the funniest.

A different player becomes Card Tsar at the start of the next round.

Awesome Point

You get an Awesome Point when you win. Go you, you're awesome.

Hand

Your set of 10 White Cards.

Play

The string of text made from the Black Card and the White Card(s) you chose.

Winner

The player whose play is chosen as funniest by the Card Tsar is the winner, and receives an Awesome Point.

Pack

A set of Black Cards and White Cards, often on a particular theme. One or more packs form a deck.

Deck

The game's Black and White Cards are drawn randomly from the deck. A deck is made from one or more packs.

As of version v0.5 all games must use the same deck. There's an open issue to have a unique deck per game.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have this in my own channel?

Yes! Just /invite the bot to your channel. /kick it out again to make it go away.

On Freenode the bot is called P_A_H.

On Blitzed the bot is called AgainstHumanity.

You'll be stuck with certain bot-wide configurations when you use someone else's bot. As of version v0.5 these include things like the turnclock and the selection of card packs. If you want for those settings to be different then you'll need to run your own bot.

Can I run this myself?

Yes! Download it from the GitHub repository. You'll need Perl and SQLite and the ability to install some Perl modules.

Why is the bot sometimes so lagged?

The bot rate limits its own messages so it doesn't get flooded off.

The bot sends a lot of text, and if it didn't rate limit itself then the IRC daemon would fairly quickly start applying anti-flood measures to it. You know that's happening because some messages just don't arrive. Eventually the IRC daemon disconnects the boot for flooding.

This can get a little confusing when the bot is saying things in the channel but appears to not be responding to commands you type in private to it. It actually has responded, but just hasn't got around to sending that message to you yet.

Please just be patient and the bot will eventually get through it all.

We're working on finding out how far we can push things. We know that having the bot voiced in the channel "helps" but don't know specifically how much. As of version v0.5 the bot sends a burst of 10 messages at one per 0.2seconds and then after that one per second until it hasn't sent any for a while again.

Can the game be played faster?

This game is designed to be slow so you can play it while you do whatever else it is you do while you're on IRC. So, if you're not the one running the bot then chances are you're using a bot configured with a turnclock of several days. This is set on purpose so that everyone playing can, if necessary, take several days to do their duty.

If you run your own bot then you can have a faster game by setting a much shorter turnclock, such as 1 hour. This works well; in fact that was how we tested this bot in the first days. The game will go as fast as the slowest player present. The main problem with it is that you need to do the work to get people all together at once and ready to play.

If no one is making the effort to organise play times then the natural asynchronous nature of IRC will eventually mean that you can't get a quorum of four players in the same place at the same time within one hour, and your game will never proceed.

That's why we run with a big turnclock: to make it work on IRC as a background activity with no need to organise.

Can the bot be made to regularly tell me the game status?

In short, no: it's not necessary.

Players new to this game are often not used to the idea of playing a game that mostly involves waiting for other people to do things. This is understandable; the traditional concept of playing a game tends to conjure up images of people together doing things in real time.

PAH is not like that. PAH is more like a play by mail sort of thing. It's asynchronous and event-driven, the events being the players taking their actions. Like IRC itself.

But it's the urge to "play this game now, come on, play it," that leads people to ask

What's happening?

What's happening?

What's happening?

What's happening now?

You can of course periodically use the status command in private to interrogate the bot about the status of the game. And so, people concerned with just what is going on here? ask for the bot to regularly spew out a status update without them having to ask for one.

Relax. Go about your serious IRC business. You were on IRC before this game existed. Continue doing that, whatever it was.

In fact the bot does inform you periodically of what is going on, it's just that it informs you only when something has actually happened. Repeatedly giving you information you already have would be unwelcome to most people, so you most likely do not really want a regular status update.

That is not to say that further features designed to gently encourage players who need to take their turn to do so would not be welcome, because they would. If they make sense and are compatible with the idea of not being hassled until you need to be hassled.. Please submit an issue!

If I quit IRC will it resign me automatically?

No. Asking to be resigned or being forcibly resigned due to timeout are the only ways.

This is intentional so that people are able to quit IRC (or get disconnected), some back and carry on playing. Apart from doing your duty, the only thing you need to do is idle in the channel.

IRC bots should use NOTICE! Why doesn't this use NOTICE!?

I know, I know. I'm a big believer in bots using NOTICE even though it seems to have fallen out of fashion these days. All my other bots try to use NOTICE exclusively.

So why not PAH? It's because it relies on the hilight feature of your IRC client to notify you when you need to do something, and hilights (in irssi) don't work in NOTICEs.

There's an issue open that asks for a mode where extra private messages in place of channel messages that would hilight people, and all other channel messags to be NOTICEs. It hasn't been tried yet, but it will be eventually, if people are still playing this game.
